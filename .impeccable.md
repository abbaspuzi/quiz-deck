## Design Context

### Users
Teams preparing for a flower-focused event use this app as a quick training deck. They are usually on phones and need a fast way to rehearse product knowledge, recognise arrangements, and build recall before event day.

### Brand Personality
Playful, bright, polished. The interface should feel energising and rewarding without becoming childish, cartoonish, pink-heavy, or generic SaaS.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a light interface with crisp floral-inspired color, clean structure, and game-like momentum. Prioritise mobile comfort, obvious right-or-left decisions, and a leaderboard that feels public and inviting from the landing page. Avoid dark UI, flat dashboard tropes, and overly feminine Mother's Day cliches.

### Design Principles
1. Make training feel like a fast, social challenge rather than a form.
2. Keep the experience bright and refined, not cute or cartoonish.
3. Prioritise decisive left-versus-right interactions with strong visual feedback.
4. Let non-authenticated users browse the leaderboard, but make login feel like joining the challenge.
5. Preserve clarity and speed on mobile first, with desktop layouts that feel intentional rather than stretched.
