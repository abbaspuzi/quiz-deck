Guide for Bloomthis MDAY QUIZ

This guide is designed for the Ops Exec / Quiz Maker tasked with designing competency assessments for Mother's Day staff. To create an effective quiz, you must focus on the specific visual cues that prevent a packer from sending out a "Zandra" for a "Zaylee" order.

1. Identify the "High-Risk Clusters"

The quiz should not test random facts. It should specifically target products that look similar to an untrained eye. Focus your questions on these four clusters:

  • Cluster A: The Russian Wraps (Marilyn vs. Miriam)
  • Cluster B: The Sunflower Hearts (Zooey Bouquet vs. Zooey Box vs. Zendaya)
  • Cluster C: The Pink Hatboxes (Tessa vs. Zandra vs. Zaylee)
  • Cluster D: The Technical Wraps (Laycie vs. Trixie vs. Mischa)

2. Use the "A vs. B" Question Logic

Effective questions for runners and packers should present a "Which is which?" scenario. Avoid general questions like "What is in a Marilyn?" Instead, use comparative logic:

  • The "Elimination" Question: "Both Marilyn and Miriam are Russian-style. If the wrapper is yellow, which one is it?"
  • The "Ingredient Audit" Question: "You see a box with Sunflowers and Red Rose Spray. Is this Zooey or Zendaya?"
  • The "Counting" Question: "If  a Zooey Box has 3 sunflowers, is it Medium or Large?"

3. The "Hero" Rule

Runners and Packers must prioritize counting the Hero Flowers (Sunflowers, Gerberas, Lilies). While filler volume (Matthiola, Eustoma) also increases with size, the Hero count is the most objective and fastest way to verify size at the QC station.

5. Guidelines for Effective Distractors

A "Distractor" is a wrong answer that sounds plausible to someone who hasn't studied.

  • Mix up the names: Use Zandra and Zinnia together in the same question because they both start with Z and are both Gerberas.
  • Mix up the centers: Use "Greenheart" as a distractor for a question about the Zooey Box.
  • Mix up the hardware: Suggest a "Black box" as a distractor, even though all Mother's Day boxes are Peach. This tests if they are paying attention to the flowers rather than the packaging.

6. Final QC Check for Station Leads

Before an order is cleared for the courier:

  1. Variety Check: Is it the right flower? (e.g., Gerbera vs. Mum).
  2. Size Check: Is the Hero count correct? (e.g., 3 vs. 5).
  3. Hardware Check: Is it in the right box? (Standard vs. XL).

7. Final Quiz Checklist (The QC for the Creator)

Before publishing the quiz to the team, ensure every question meets these criteria:

  1. Is it Visual? Does the question describe something the packer can actually see (color, count, shape)?
  2. Is it Decisive? Is there only one correct answer based on the unique identifiers?
  3. Is it Practical? Does it solve a real-world mistake likely to happen on the production line?

Question Examples


Category 1: Basic Product Knowledge (Visual "Tells")

Focus: Instant identification via wrappers, ribbons, and hero colors.

1. You see a bouquet with a Purple wrapper on the line. Which SKU is it?

  • Answer: Trixie.

  • Identifier: Trixie is the only Mother's Day SKU with a purple exterior.

2. A bouquet features a cream wrapper with 3 pink rubber ribbons attached directly to the paper. Which SKU is this?

  • Answer: Laycie.

  • Identifier: The 3-ribbon count on the cream wrap is a unique technical identifier.

3. Which Russian-style bouquet uses a yellow wrapper and a white ribbon?

  • Answer: Miriam.

  • Identifier: Miriam (Yellow/White) vs. Marilyn (Pink/Black).

Category 2: Size & Scale Check (Counting)

Focus: Distinguishing between Medium, Large, and Luxe variants.

4. A Marilyn LARGE bouquet should have how many Pink Gerberas?

  • Answer: 5 Gerberas.

  • Identifier: Medium (M) = 3 stems; Large (L) = 5 stems.

5. If a Zooey bouquet contains only 3 Sunflowers, which size should be marked on the order tag?

  • Answer: Medium (M).

  • Identifier: 3 stems = Medium; 5 stems = Large.

6. For a Beatrice LUXE order, what is the required hardware and hero lily count?

  • Answer: XL Hatbox and 5 Lilies.

  • Identifier: Standard = 3 Lilies in a Standard box; Luxe = 5 Lilies in an XL box.

Category 3: Scenario-Based (Floor Verification)

Focus: Resolving high-risk confusion points in a live production environment.

7. Scenario: A runner brings a Peach Hatbox to the QC station. It contains Greenheart Sunflowers and Red Rose Spray. Which SKU is this?

  • A) Zooey Box

  • B) Zendaya Box

  • Answer: B) Zendaya Box.

  • Reason: While both can use Greenheart centers, only Zendaya includes the Red Rose Spray.

8. Scenario: You are auditing a Marilyn Russian bouquet. You notice White Matthiola has been added as a filler. Is this correct?

  • Answer: No.

  • Reason: Marilyn is specifically characterized by the absence of White Matthiola. If Matthiola is present, it is likely a Miriam or an assembly error.

9. Scenario: A packer has two peach hatboxes. Box A has 3 Anthuriums and Lilac Spidermums. Box B has 1 Anthurium and Pink Gerberas. Identify both.

  • Answer: Box A is Zaylee; Box B is Ayame.

  • Reason: The Anthurium count (3 vs. 1) is the decisive factor between these two.

Category 4: Multi-Ingredient Audit (COMPLEX)

Focus: Complexity checks for mixed bouquets.

10. What are the key components of the Laycie Lilac Rose Bouquet?

a) Ocean Song Rose, White Phalaenopsis Orchid, White Matthiola, Blue Eryngium, Snow Willow

b) Ocean Song Rose, Pink Anthurium, White Statice, Thlaspi, Eucalyptus

c) Lilac Rose, White Lily, Pink Eustoma, Blue Eryngium, Snow Willow

d) Ocean Song Rose, White Matthiola, Pink Phlox, Blue Eryngium, Eucalyptus

  • Correct Answer: A

  • QC Tip: This is our most premium lilac bouquet; look for the "Luxe" touch of the Phalaenopsis orchid and the unique blue eryngium.

11. To correctly identify the Zaylee Flower Box, which ingredients must be present?

a) Lilac Spidermum, Light Peach Carnation, 2-3 Anthuriums, Eucalyptus

b) Pink Gerbera, Lilac Spidermum, 1 Anthurium, Ruscus

c) Lilac Spidermum, Hot Cherry Pink Rose, 2-3 Anthuriums, Eucalyptus

d) Pink Gerbera, Hot Cherry Pink Rose, 1 Anthurium, Ruscus

  • Correct Answer: A

  • QC Tip: Remember Zaylee has 0 Gerberas. If you see a Gerbera, it is likely a Zandra or Ayame.

12. What specific mix defines the Zooey Sunflower Mother's Day Bouquet?

a) Greenheart Sunflower, Alstroemeria, Oxypetalum, Eucalyptus b) Blackheart Sunflower, Red Rose Spray, White Statice, Ruscus c) Greenheart Sunflower, Red Rose Spray, Oxypetalum, Thlaspi d) Blackheart Sunflower, Alstroemeria, White Matthiola, Eucalyptus

  • Correct Answer: A

  • QC Tip: The Zooey Bouquet must use the Greenheart center and the specific pairing of Alstroemeria and Oxypetalum.

13. Which of the following best describes the ingredient list for the Miriam Russian Bouquet?

a) Pink Carnation, White Matthiola, Pale Pink Roses, Eucalyptus b) Hot Cherry Pink Rose, Pink Carnation, White Statice, Thlaspi c) Pink Carnation, White Eustoma, 2-3 Anthuriums, Snow Willow d) Pink Rose, White Matthiola, Light Pink Carnation Spray, Thlaspi

  • Correct Answer: A

  • QC Tip: Miriam is distinguished by its paler palette and the inclusion of Matthiola, unlike the Marilyn.

Category 7: Orchid Identification & Stalk Counts

Focus: Potted plants vs. cut flowers, and differentiating the Indira SKU.

Identification by Stalk Count (Standard Orchids)

  • 33. Question: 

Which SKU is this?

  • Answer: Mother's Day Pink Phalaenopsis Orchid (2 stalks).
  • Identifier: Standard orchids come in 1-stalk or 2-stalk options. They are easily identified by the plain white pot without extra decorative wrapping.

The "Indira" Unique Identifier

  • 34. Question: 
  • What is the SKU name?
  • Answer: Indira Pink & White Mother's Day Phalaenopsis Orchid.
  • Identifier: The Indira is the only orchid SKU in this set that uses mixed colors (pink and white) and features the signature pink decorative base wrap.

Scenario: Mixed Orchid Verification

  • Scenario: You are auditing an orchid pot. It has three stalks, but all of them are pink, and it is in a plain white pot with no decorative wrapping. Is this an Indira?
  • Answer: No.
  • Reason: An Indira must have a mix of pink and white stalks AND the pink decorative wrap around the pot. A 3-stalk pink-only arrangement in a plain pot would be a production error.

35. What specific ingredients make up the Indira Pink & White Orchid arrangement? 

a) 3 Stalks (Mixed Pink & White), White Pot, Pink Decorative Cloth Wrap, Accent flowers at base

b) 2 Stalks (All Pink), White Pot, No Wrap, Eucalyptus

c) 3 Stalks (All White), White Pot, Pink Ribbon, Moss

d) 4 Stalks (Mixed Pink & White), Glass Vase, Pink Decorative Cloth Wrap, Accent flowers

  • Correct Answer: A
  • QC Tip: The Indira is the "Luxe" version of the orchids. Always look for the pink fabric around the pot and the mixed stalk colors.
